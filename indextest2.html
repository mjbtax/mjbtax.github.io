INCORPORATION (SECTION 85 ASSET ROLLOVER)

An entrepreneur commonly starts their business in a partnership or sole proprietorship, they take advantage of start-up losses.

Soon the business is profitable, and then incorporation.

A transfer of a sole proprietorship or a partnership to a corporation occurs under section 85.

The provision under Section 85 for the rollover of capital assets can be used for many types of re-organization.

Normally the disposition of property produces the recognition of taxable gain or loss by the taxpayer.

However you can defer the immediate tax liability through a transfer per Section 85.

Many first time entrepreneurs completed a Section 85 and have no concern of the potential tax liability.

Another scenario that can come years later arises when being denied access to the Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption on the sale of the shares of your business.

The LCGE enables taxpayers tax free profit of up to $1.9 million of capital gains (as of 2020).

Ensure you have your business properly structured, and that you too can access the LCGE.

We have the expertise to review if this has been completed correctly for your business.
